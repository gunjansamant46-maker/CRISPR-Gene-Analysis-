# Gene Background: TP53

TP53 is a tumor suppressor gene that encodes the p53 protein. The p53 protein plays an essential role in maintaining genomic stability by regulating cell cycle arrest, DNA repair mechanisms, and apoptosis.

Because of its role in preventing the proliferation of damaged cells, TP53 is often referred to as the "guardian of the genome."

## Genomic Location

Chromosome: 17  
Cytogenetic location: 17p13.1  
Reference sequence: NC_000017.11

The cytogenetic location 17p13.1 refers to the short arm (p) of chromosome 17 in region 1, band 3, sub-band 1.

## Gene Structure

The TP53 gene contains multiple exons that encode the p53 protein. Alternative splicing allows the gene to produce multiple transcript variants.

Understanding gene structure is important when studying gene mutations and designing genome editing experiments.

## Biological Importance

TP53 is among the most frequently mutated genes in human cancers. When the gene is mutated, the p53 protein loses its ability to regulate cell growth and DNA repair, allowing abnormal cells to divide uncontrollably.
