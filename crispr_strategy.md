# Conceptual CRISPR Strategy

CRISPR-Cas9 is a genome editing technology that allows precise modification of DNA sequences within the genome.

## Relevance to TP53

Because TP53 plays a central role in cancer biology, genome editing technologies such as CRISPR are frequently used in research to study the effects of specific mutations.

Researchers can use CRISPR to:

- Introduce specific TP53 mutations in cell models
- Study the functional impact of those mutations
- Investigate how TP53 influences DNA repair and cell cycle regulation

## Challenges in Genome Editing

- Off-target mutations
- Efficient delivery of CRISPR components
- Ethical considerations in genome editing research

Despite these challenges, CRISPR remains one of the most powerful tools for studying gene function and disease mechanisms.
